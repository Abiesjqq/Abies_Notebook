## Maximum matching and minimum vertex cover:

**Maximum Matching**

$$
\begin{aligned}
    \max \ &\sum_{e \in E} x_e \\
    \text{s.t.} \ &\sum_{e = (u, v) \in E}x_e \leq 1\quad (\forall u \in V) \\
    \ & x_e \geq 0\quad (\forall e \in E)
\end{aligned}
$$

and **Minimum vertex cover**

$$
\begin{aligned}
    \min\  &\sum_{u \in V}x_u \\
    \text{s.t.}\  & x_u + x_v \geq 1 \quad (\forall (u, v) \in E) \\
    \ & x_u \geq 0
\end{aligned}
$$

They are dual! But we can not say that they are equivalent since the optimal may be fractional!

!!! remarks "In bipartite graphs"
    For bipartite graphs, there always exist optimal integral LP solutions.

## Maximum flow and minimum cut

$$
\begin{aligned}
    \max\ &\sum_{u:(s, u) \in E}f_{su} \\
    \text{s.t.}\ &0 \leq f_{uv} \leq c_{uv} \quad (\forall (u, v) \in E) \\
    \ & \sum_{v:(v, u) \in E}f_{vu} = \sum_{w:(u, w) \in E}f_{uw} \quad (\forall u \in V \setminus \{s, t\})
\end{aligned}
$$

i.e.

$$
\begin{aligned}
    \max\quad 
        & \sum_{u:(s,u)\in E} f_{su} \\[4pt]
    \text{s.t.}\quad
        & f_{uv} \le c_{uv} 
            \quad (\forall (u,v)\in E) \\
        & \sum_{v:(v,u)\in E} f_{vu}
          - \sum_{w:(u,w)\in E} f_{uw}
          \le 0
            \quad (\forall u\in V\setminus\{s,t\}) \\[6pt]
        & - \sum_{v:(v,u)\in E} f_{vu}
          + \sum_{w:(u,w)\in E} f_{uw}
          \le 0
            \quad (\forall u\in V\setminus\{s,t\}) \\[6pt]
        & f_{uv} \ge 0
            \quad (\forall (u,v)\in E)
\end{aligned}
$$

in standard form.  
The dual form turns out to be 

$$
\begin{aligned}
    \min\quad 
        & \sum_{(u,v)\in E} c_{uv}\, y_{uv} \\[6pt]
    \text{s.t.}\quad
        & y_{su} + z_u \ge 1 
            \quad && \forall\, u:(s,u)\in E \\[6pt]
        & y_{vt} - z_v \ge 0
            \quad && \forall\, v:(v,t)\in E \\[6pt]
        & y_{uv} - z_u + z_v \ge 0
            \quad && \forall\, (u,v)\in E,\ u\neq s,\ v\neq t \\[6pt]
        & y_{uv} \ge 0
            \quad && \forall\, (u,v)\in E
\end{aligned}
$$

If we let $y_{uv}$ describes if edge $(u, v)$ is cut ($1$ is cut); $z_u$ describes $u$'s _side_ ($1$ on $s$ side), it describes a **minimum cut problem**!

Then we gonna prove that an LP solution of it ($\text{OPT}$) will correspond to a cut.

Firstly, let $\{L^*, R^*\}$ be a min cut, then 

$$
\mathrm{OPT} \leq \sum_{(u, v) \in E}c_{uv}y_{uv} = \sum_{\substack{(u, v) \in E \\ u \in L^*, v \in R^*}} = c(L^*, R^*)
$$

!!! normal-comment "Total Unimodular Matrix and relative theorem"

    A matrix $\boldsymbol{A}$ is **totally unimodular** if every square submatrix has determinant is $0, 1, -1$.

    **Theorem** If $\boldsymbol{A} \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ is totally unimodular and $\boldsymbol{b}$, then the polytope $P = \{\boldsymbol{x}\ |\ \boldsymbol{Ax} \leq \boldsymbol{b}, \boldsymbol{x} \geq \boldsymbol{0}\}$ has integer vertices.

If $\boldsymbol{A}$ is totally unimodular, then so are $(\boldsymbol{A}, \boldsymbol{I}), (\boldsymbol{A}, \boldsymbol{I})^T$;  
If $(\boldsymbol{A}, \boldsymbol{I}), (\boldsymbol{A}, \boldsymbol{I})^T, (\boldsymbol{I}, \boldsymbol{A}), (\boldsymbol{I}, \boldsymbol{A})^T, \boldsymbol{A}^T$ are all unimodular, then so is $\boldsymbol{A}$.

!!! remarks "Proof"

    If $\boldsymbol{v} \in \mathbb{R}^n$ is a vertex of $P$, then there exists an invertible square submatrix $\boldsymbol{A}'$ such that $\boldsymbol{A}'\boldsymbol{v} = \boldsymbol{b}$, then $\boldsymbol{v} = \boldsymbol{A}'^{-1}\boldsymbol{b}$ is all integer because $\boldsymbol{A}'^{-1}$ is integer!
